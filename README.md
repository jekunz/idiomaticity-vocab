# How idiomatic is synthetic instruction-tuning data? A vocabulary-based exploration

## Step 1: Get Over- and Underrepresented Words in a Back-Translation Corpus

You will compare corpora to find words that occur proportionally more often in one than in another, looking for traces of *translationese*.

You have three corpora:

- **Original** — running text (txt) from FineWeb-2.
- **OPUS-MT back-translation** — the same sentences, translated to English and back via OPUS-MT. 
- **Gemma3 back-translation** — the same sentences, back-translated via the LLM Gemma3.

Run the comparison twice: original vs. OPUS-MT, and original vs. Gemma3.

---

### Method

(suggestion for code is in `analyze_backtranslation.py`; adjust as needed)

Do the following for each comparison (original vs. one back-translation corpus). (Steps 2--5 are similar to the course project.)

**1. Tokenize.** Use the same tokenization for all corpora. Lowercase the text, treat letter sequences (including å, ä, ö, é, ü) as words, and exclude punctuation and digits. (You can use the regex tokenizer from Lab 1 and exclude punctuation and digits.)

**2. Count** how often each word occurs in each corpus.

**3. Relative frequency** = word count / total number of words in the corpus (total counted after excluding punctuation and digits).

**4. Ratio** = relative frequency in the original / relative frequency in the back-translation.
- ratio > 1 → overrepresented in the original
- ratio < 1 → overrepresented in the back-translation

**5. Threshold.** Keep only words occurring at least 10 times in *both* corpora.

---

### Candidate lists

For each comparison, produce two lists:

- sorted by **descending** ratio → most overrepresented in the original
- sorted by **ascending** ratio → most overrepresented in the back-translation

Save as CSV with columns: `word, count_original, count_bt, relfreq_original, relfreq_bt, ratio`.

These are *candidate* lists and still contain English words and other stuff to be removed by hand. Don't cut them at 100; export enough extra so that 100 valid Swedish words remain after filtering.

(candidate lists generated by me are in `lists_raw`)

---

### Manual review

Go through each candidate list from the top and keep going until you have **100 Swedish words**. With two comparisons this gives four lists (over- and underrepresented, for OPUS-MT and Gemma3).

Discard anything that isn't a Swedish word: English and other foreign words, nonsense, fragments, tokenization artifacts. For ambiguous cases, decide a rule and apply it the same way everywhere. Keep a record of what you discarded.

## Step 2: Look for the Words in the Instruction-Tuning Data

Check if the words are in `magpie_swe_annotation.json`:

- **Count** each word's occurrences in the file, using the same tokenization and lowercasing as before.
- Are there more occurrences of the words overrepresentad in the original or in the backtranslation data? What do you observe? 
- **Even vs. odd.** The samples fall into two groups, one with even and one with odd indices. Compare the counts between the groups. Are there any notable differences? (I will tell you what exactly they are later, but can't right now because I don't want to bias the other annotation :))
- **Translationese.** Counts show *how often* words occur, not whether a use is idiomatic. Pull up the surrounding context for some occurrences, judge by eye, and write down notes.
